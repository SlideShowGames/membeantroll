When something bad or painful abates, it becomes less strong or severe.

If you alleviate pain or suffering, you make it less intense or severe.

Something that is arduous is extremely difficult; hence, it involves a lot of effort.

The cessation of a process is a stop or halt to it.

A chronic illness or pain is serious and lasts for a long time; a chronic problem is always happening or returning and is very difficult to solve.

If someone is granted clemency, they are punished less severely than they could have been—they have been shown mercy.

When someone is exonerated from guilt, a task, or a responsibility, they are set free or excused from it.

A hiatus is a period of time when there is a break or interruption in some activity.

Something that is immutable is always the same and cannot be changed.

Something that is interminable continues for a very long time in a boring or annoying way.

An irrevocable action or decision is impossible to change, reverse, or stop.

A laborious job or process takes a long time, requires a lot of effort, and is often boring.

If you mitigate something that causes harm, you reduce the harmful or painful effects of it.

If you mollify someone, you say or do something to make that person feel less angry or upset.

Something that is perennial lasts a very long time and is enduring.

A reprieve is a temporary relief from or cancellation of a punishment; it can also be a relief from something, such as pain or trouble of some kind.

If you are steadfast, you have a firm belief in your actions or opinions and refuse to give up or change them because you are certain that you are doing the right thing.

A tenacious person does not quit until they finish what they’ve started.
